Homeklin (U) Proposed National Project

Establishing modern waste-to-energy hubs that collect waste and convert it into biogas and agricultural by-products across Uganda.

Project Overview

The project will establish modern waste energy hubs that collect and convert waste into biogas and agricultural by-products. These hubs will serve as centers of excellence for circular economy practices, transforming Uganda's waste crisis into an opportunity for sustainable development, clean energy production, and agricultural prosperity.

General Objectives

Improve Waste Collection Coverage & Frequency

Extend waste collection services to underserved communities and ensure regular, reliable pickup schedules.

Increase Community Awareness & Participation

Educate citizens on proper waste segregation, recycling benefits, and the value of circular economy practices.

Promote Organic Farming Using Natural Manure

Encourage farmers to adopt liquid fertilizer and bio-slurry to enhance soil health and crop yields sustainably.

Promote Zero Dumping & Zero Landfilling

Eliminate illegal dumping sites and transition toward a circular model where all waste is reused or recycled.

Company Responsibilities & Specific Objectives

Infrastructure Development
  • Construct and operate waste-to-energy processing hubs
  • Install biogas digesters and liquid fertilizer production units
  • Establish waste collection logistics and sorting facilities
Operational Excellence
  • Process 500+ tonnes of organic waste daily
  • Produce affordable biogas for households and institutions
  • Generate high-quality organic liquid fertilizer for farmers
Community & Stakeholder Engagement
  • Partner with KCCA for city-wide waste management
  • Conduct regular community training and awareness campaigns
  • Create job opportunities for local youth and women
Environmental Stewardship
  • Achieve zero dumping and zero landfilling targets
  • Reduce greenhouse gas emissions from waste decomposition
  • Promote sustainable agriculture through organic fertilizer
